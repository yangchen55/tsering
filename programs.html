    • LANGUAGE PROGRAMS

    The preservation of one's identity and culture comes from the preservation of one's language. Buddhist philosophy is ingrained in the traditions and culture of the Himalayan communities. To ensure that the Tibetan language and social values are not lost as young and old migrate to new locales, the HSYWE offers free, informal Tibetan-language and Special Buddhist Scripture Reading programs. An English-language program is also offered to helo members succeed in today's global marketplace.

    Students range in age from the elderly to teenagers and come from all walks of life. Monks learn alongside housewives. Everyone is welcome regardless of their education level. These sessions are taught by a dedicated team of full-time teachers and volunteers from all over the world.

    Tibetan-language Program

    This program is taught in Tibetan. Students will be given special text books published by the HSYWE. The customized text books were developed to support the organisation's cultural-preservation mission.

    • Basic I and II: Students will learn how to read, spell and write Tibetan script including up to 1,000 new vocabulary words. Both U-Chen and U-Mey handwriting will be introduced. Text book: Bhokyi Che Kaed Lop Dhep Dangpo (General Tibetan Language - Book One). Duration: 4-5 months per level
    • Advanced I and II: More complex vocabulary, grammar, sentence construction, and letter functions will be introduced. Students will practice reading and comprehending short stories regarding Tibetan traditions and culture. U-May handwriting will be taught. Text book: Bhokyi Che Kaed Lob Dhep Nyipa (General Tibetan Language - Book Two). Duration: 5-6 months per level. Prerequisite: Basic I and II.

    Special Buddhist Scripture Reading

    This program is for individuals who want to learn how to read Buddhist scripture. Instruction is in Tibetan, and scripture writing is not taught. Students will be given a unique text book published bu the HSYWE which was specially developed to support the organisation's objectives to preserve Tibetan culture. The text book features well-researched explanations for each scripture including its origin and meaning. Text Book: Book of Prayers

    • Foundation: This is a basic introduction to the alphabet, vowels, phonetics and pronunciation. Duration: 8 months
    • Levels I-IV: Students will be introduced to different TIbetan-Buddhist scriptures such as Dolma, Kyabdo, Lhamo, Lhagu, Sherab Nyingpo, Bharche Namsel, and more. Duration: 8 months per level

    English-Language Program


    • Basic I - A student's first indroduction to the English language, this course teaches the fundamentals including yhr alphabet, basic grammar, subject-specific vocabulary, and simple conversational skills. Duration: 5 months.
    • Basic II - Students will spend the majority of time revisiting the skills they learned in the Basic I course. More advanced vocabulary, grammar, reading, and conversational skills will be taught and short-composition writing will be briefly introduced. Duration:5-6 months. Prerequisite: Basic I.
    • Intermediate - The focus of this course is to ensure that students can confidently use all the vocabulary and grammar taught in the Basic I and II programs in brief conversations and compositions. Duration: 5 months. Prerequisites: Basic I and II
    • Advanced I: Students will learn more complex vocabulary, conversation, and composition-writing skills and will apply them to a wide range of topics. Duration: 6 months. Prerequisites: Basic I, Basic II and intermediate.
    • Advanced II: Students will work on mastering the skills they have learned in the previous levels and will be able to read, write and converse with ease. Duration: 6 months. Prerequisites: Basic I, Basic II, Advanced I and intermediate.

    Awareness Programs

    The HSYWE is dedicated to serving not only students, but also the wider community. A variety of free awareness programs geared toward cultural-heritage preservation and personal growth are offered to all ages.

    Buddhist Philosophy

    Every Saturday (9:00-11:00 AM) the HSYWE offers an introductory program on Buddhist Philosophy. Different topics are taught each week by a Geshe or Lopon from Kopan Monastery, Sakya Monastery, Shechen Tennyi Dargyeling Monastery and Thrangu Tashi Choling Monastery. The program is very popular among all ages.

    Scholarships

    The HSYWE offers scholarships for higher education and vocational training to high-achieving, financially disadvantaged youth and women within Nepal's Himalayan-ethnic communities, especially Tibetans. The amount of monetary assistance granted is based on the financial need of each scholarships recipient and the HSYWE's available funds. The progress of each scholarship student is evaluated on a regular basis
